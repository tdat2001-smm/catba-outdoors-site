Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before visiting Cat Ba

Getting Here

Cat Ba Island is accessible from Hanoi (about 3.5 hours) or Hai Phong (about 1.5 hours). You can take a bus + ferry combo from Hanoi, or drive to Hai Phong and take the high-speed ferry from Got pier. We can arrange transport for you — just ask!

Yes! We offer transfers from Hanoi (Noi Bai Airport or Old Quarter), Hai Phong, and Cat Bi Airport. Private car + ferry combos are available. Contact us for pricing and scheduling.

The best time is from April to October for warm weather and clear skies. September–November can have some rain but fewer crowds. December–March is cooler but still beautiful for hiking and cultural experiences.

Tours & Activities

You can book directly on our website through any tour page, or contact us via Zalo, WhatsApp, or email. We'll confirm your booking within 2 hours and send you all the details you need.

Free cancellation up to 24 hours before your tour. Cancellations within 24 hours may be subject to a 50% fee. Weather-related cancellations are always fully refundable or rescheduled at no cost.

Most of our tours welcome children aged 5 and above. Some activities like rock climbing have age/weight restrictions. We'll advise on the best options for families when you book.

We recommend: sunscreen, hat, comfortable shoes, swimwear, towel, camera, and a light rain jacket. We provide all necessary equipment (kayaks, life jackets, climbing gear, etc.).

Payment

We accept: bank transfer (VietQR), international credit/debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, JCB) via VTCPay, and cash (VND or USD). Online payment is confirmed automatically.

Prices are displayed in USD for international guests. VND equivalent is also shown. You can pay in either currency. The exchange rate is updated daily.

For most day tours, no deposit is required — just show up! For multi-day experiences (floating homestay, custom trips), we ask for a 30% deposit to secure your booking.

Stays

The Healing Camp is a seaside camping experience — not a hotel. You'll sleep in comfortable tents or pods by the ocean, eat mindful meals at our kitchen, and enjoy the bar, bonfire nights, and wellness activities. It's designed for people who want to slow down.

Absolutely. Our floating houses are well-maintained and stable. Life jackets are provided. You'll be accompanied by experienced local hosts who know the bay intimately. We've been hosting guests safely for years.

The Healing Camp has Wi-Fi in common areas. The floating homestay has limited connectivity — that's part of the experience! We recommend embracing the digital detox.
